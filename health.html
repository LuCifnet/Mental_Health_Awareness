What Is Depression?

Depression is a common but serious mood disorder that affects how you feel, think, and handle daily activities. It causes feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and a lack of interest in things once enjoyed.

Types of Depression: 15 Common Depressive Disorders

  • Major Depressive Disorder
  • Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia)
  • Bipolar Disorder
  • Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)
  • Postpartum Depression
  • Psychotic Depression
  • Atypical Depression
  • Cyclothymic Disorder
  • Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder
  • Situational Depression
  • Disruptive Mood Dysregulation Disorder
  • Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)
  • Recurrent Depression
  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
  • Borderline Personality Disorder

Depression Symptoms, Signs, & What to Watch For

  • Persistent sadness or emptiness
  • Loss of interest in previously enjoyable activities
  • Fatigue and lack of energy
  • Feelings of worthlessness or guilt
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Sleep disturbances (insomnia or excessive sleeping)
  • Thoughts of death or suicide

Depression: Types, Symptoms, & Treatments

There are various types of depression, each with different symptoms and treatments. Treatments include medication, therapy, lifestyle changes, and in some cases, hospitalization.

What Does Depression Feel Like?

Depression often feels like a weight on your chest, preventing you from moving forward. You may feel overwhelmed, disconnected from others, or like you’re trapped in a constant cycle of sadness and fatigue.

Frequently Asked Questions About Depression

Common Types of Depression

There are many types of depression, each with its own symptoms and treatment options. Some of the most common include Major Depressive Disorder, Bipolar Disorder, and Seasonal Affective Disorder.

Is It Depression Or Something Else?

If you're unsure whether you're experiencing depression or another condition, it's important to consult a healthcare professional. Symptoms of depression can overlap with other conditions, including anxiety disorders and chronic illnesses.

Situations That Can Cause Depression

Stressful life events such as the loss of a loved one, a difficult breakup, financial problems, or job loss can trigger depressive episodes. Chronic health conditions, trauma, or substance abuse can also contribute to depression.
